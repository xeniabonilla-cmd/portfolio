Strategic Account Retention  ·  Executive Relationship Management  ·  Revenue Protection
Defending a flagship national account against two years of competitive pressure — zero attrition
0%
Share of wallet lost

Situation

Within my largest national account — a flagship relationship spanning a geographically distributed U.S. footprint and representing significant portfolio revenue — one key site became a high-risk retention point. Local leadership was actively evaluating alternative suppliers. In accounts of this size, one lost location rarely stays one.

What I Did

I designed and executed a long-term retention strategy built on accountability and relationship depth. I developed structured corrective action plans, tracked performance rigorously, and made repeated executive-level site visits to position myself — and the company — as a committed strategic partner. Across two years I managed layered communication between site leadership, corporate stakeholders, and internal teams, keeping all parties aligned and the relationship forward-moving.

Result

Zero attrition. Through sustained competitive evaluation and two years of pressure, the account never switched suppliers. One of my highest-value relationships remained protected — held not by contract leverage, but by the kind of trust that only consistent, high-touch partnership earns.

Relationship Recovery  ·  Strategic Onboarding  ·  Cultural Competency
Rebuilding a damaged national account from the ground up — 5 of 6 locations converting to primary supplier within 6 months
83%
Conversion rate, damaged account

Situation

A nationally significant account had been damaged by prior mismanagement, leaving the relationship fractured and trust low. When the account was re-awarded, locations weren't obligated to source exclusively through us — they could pull from a pool of approved suppliers. We had a name to rebuild. I was brought in to make sure we earned their business, not just held the contract.

What I Did

I developed a structured onboarding strategy focused on relationship-building at the local level — not just account-level coordination. I created detailed plans to ensure our regional teams developed their own direct relationships with site contacts, built in monthly then quarterly reporting cadences, and monitored every location through the transition. I prioritized cultural alignment throughout, ensuring our team engaged this partner with the respect and attentiveness the relationship required.

Result

Within 6 months, 5 of 6 locations — including one site operating across two distribution warehouses — were sourcing primarily through us. What had been a damaged relationship became one of my strongest accounts, built on consistency, structure, and genuine partnership.

"I manage accounts by understanding the business behind the contact — and the people behind the business."
My approach centers on long-term relationship health over short-term wins. I invest early in understanding each client's internal pressures, strategic priorities, and decision-making structure — so when challenges arise, I'm already positioned as a partner, not a vendor. Fluency in both English and Spanish allows me to build trust across cultural contexts that many account managers simply can't access.
